SAPPHIRES

Cartier 2015

The Sapphire in the center weighs 197.80 carats.

The Sapphire's first owner, Maria Feodorovna (1847-1928) , queen of the Russian House of Romanov Alexander III of Russia, was made into a brooch surrounded by diamonds. The Sapphire disappeared after the demise of the House of Romanov until 1928, when it was remade as a necklace and sold to its second owner, Polish opera singer Ganawska Ganna Walska, 1887-1984. The Sapphire was resold in 1992, and in 2015 it was restored to the current bracelet, which cartier named "Romanov" (Romanoff) .
